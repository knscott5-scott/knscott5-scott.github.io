Let's Discuss The Basics

There are many different terms and definitions that are commonly used in the healthcare industry. It can be overwhelming to remember them all and what they mean. Below you will find a chart of definitions for some of the most common healthcare terms.

Term Definition More Resources
Premium The amount you pay for your health insurance every month. Learn More
Deductible The amount you pay for covered healthcare services before your insurance plan starts to pay. Learn More
Copayment (Copay) A fixed amount you pay for a covered healthcare service, usually at the time of service. Learn More
Coinsurance The percentage of costs you pay for a covered healthcare service after you've paid your deductible. Learn More
Out-of-Pocket Maximum The most you have to pay for covered healthcare services in a plan year. After you reach this amount, your insurance will cover 100% of the costs. Learn More
Pre-existing Condition A health condition that existed before the start of your health insurance coverage. Learn More
Step-Therapy A process where your insurance company requires you to try one treatment option before approving a more expensive or invasive procedure. Learn More
Prior Authorization A requirement that your healthcare provider obtain approval from your insurance company before providing a specific service or medication to ensure it is covered under your plan. Learn More
Quantity Limit The maximum amount of a specific medication or service that your insurance company will cover within a certain time period. Learn More
In Network Healthcare providers and facilities that have a contract with your insurance company to provide services at negotiated rates. Learn More
Out of Network Healthcare providers and facilities that do not have a contract with your insurance company. You may have to pay more for services received from out-of-network providers. Learn More
Formulary A list of prescription drugs covered by your insurance plan. It may include both generic and brand-name medications. Learn More
Open Enrollment The period during which you can enroll in a health insurance plan, make changes to your existing plan, or cancel your coverage. Learn More
Special Enrollment Period A time outside of the open enrollment period when you can enroll in a health insurance plan or make changes to your existing plan due to certain qualifying life events, such as marriage, birth of a child, or loss of other coverage. Learn More
PPO A type of health insurance plan that allows you to see both in-network and out-of-network healthcare providers, but typically offers lower costs for in-network care. Learn More
HMO A type of health insurance plan that requires you to choose a primary care physician and get referrals to see specialists. You typically must receive care from in-network providers. Learn More

Understanding these terms can help you make informed decisions about your healthcare and insurance options. If you have any questions or need further clarification, please don't hesitate to reach out to us.
