Tips and Tricks of the Trade

When looking for health insurance, either on your own or with a professional, you should always have the following information readily available.

  • A pen and paper to take notes
  • A list of any questions or concerns you may have
  • A list of all your medication with dosages and frequencies
  • A list of all doctors that you would like to keep in-network. It is helpful to have the first and last name of all doctors rather than facility names.
  • You should always mention your preferred hospital and preferred pharmacy.
  • Mention any health conditions or upcoming surgeries you may have, that way we know how to mitigate your out of pocket expenses.
  • Income information (pay stubs, tax returns, etc.) to see if you are eligible for any tax credit or medicaid

If you do seek out a professional, make sure to ask about their experience and credentials. Research their background and read reviews from previous clients.

How to Avoid Scams

Be wary of anyone who promises guaranteed approval or makes unrealistic claims about their services. Always verify the credentials and legitimacy of any insurance broker or agent you are considering working with. Always research independently. Many agents will try to use pressure tactics to push their services, it is important you do your due diligence. It also helps to have a family member or friend accompany you on calls as well. Another helpful tip is to avoid giving out your personal information, such as your social security number or bank account information, until you have thoroughly vetted the person or company you are working with. Always ask for references and check them before making any decisions, especially if they are calling you about insurance. Always verify the phone number.
